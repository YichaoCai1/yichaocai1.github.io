Yichao Cai  (蔡逸超)

I am a third-year Ph.D. student at the Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML), University of Adelaide, advised by Prof. Javen Qinfeng Shi, Dr Zhen Zhang, and Dr Yuhang Liu. Before starting my Ph.D., I spent four years working in industry. I earned both my B.E. and M.S. degrees from Wuhan University of Technology (WUT), where I was advised by Prof. Xiao Zhou. During my master’s studies, I was a visiting student researcher at Berkeley DeepDrive, UC Berkeley, working with Dr Ching-Yao Chan.

My research focuses on multimodal representation learning and principled approaches to disentanglement. I am currently exploring identifiable modeling, causal reasoning, and the use of language as an inductive signal. Looking ahead, I aim to develop explainable AI systems that can reliably uncover and represent the semantic structures underlying complex data.
