Research Overview

A central theme of my doctoral research is understanding how multimodal representations are formed, especially under contrastive objectives and language supervision. I study the geometry of contrastive learning, cross-modal alignment and misalignment, and how identifiable or interpretable structure can emerge in vision-language models.

The projects below summarize this line of work, from language-guided representation disentanglement to cross-modal misalignment and theoretical analyses of contrastive learning.
